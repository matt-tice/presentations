* Hopf Algebras

** Historical

*** Original

This is the paper which begins the study of Hopf algebras
- Hopf looks at the cohomology of H-spaces (which he refers to as homology, which is a bit confusing; but he says it's a ring, so it has to be the cohomology)
- Using the contravariant cohomology functor (he doesn't think of it as that, but still), the product on an H-space gives a coproduct on the cohomology
- Hopf studies this coproduct to get at his results
- He shows that the coproduct is compatible with the cup product (the product on the cohomology)
- Later authors would axiomatize this structure into the modern concept of Hopf algebras.

**** Notes

The title translates to
/On the Topologie of Group Manifolds and their Generalizations/

Liesche Gruppen is the term for Lie Group, and he uses /kontinuierlichen
Gruppe/ for topological group

He generalizes group manifolds to \Gamma-Mannigfaltigkeiten (these are what we call H-spaces today)

On a *closed, oriented* manifold with continuous multiplication, you can project down to the left and right multiplication by any fixed point p, to get a continuous map which depends continuously on p, say
\lambda_p(q) = pq

You consider the degree of the mapping \lambda_p ; because it depends continuously on p, all these lambdas have the same degree, which he calls c_\lambda. \Gamma-manifolds are manifolds with multiplication such that
c_\lambda and c_\rho are both \ne 0.

For any closed, oriented Lie group, looking at \lambda_1 and \rho_1 shows you that c_\lambda = c_\rho = 1.

Now, Hopf wants to look at the (co)homology of manifolds. He considers Q-coefficients, looks at the cohomology ring, with Betty-addition and intersection product.
- his main theorem is that the homology of a \Gamma-manifold is isomorphic (weakly equivalent?) to that of a product of spheres
